Music App Redesign

Redesign Spotify to make the Playlist management more user friendly.

The aim of this project is to redesigning major streaming mobile apps. Working in a group of three, we focused improving the flow of creating, managing, and running playlists.

The first step was interviewing users who used different streaming mobile apps. The interviews consists of asking interviewees how to perform a certain scenario (Creating a playlist, adding/deleting a song, etc.) to see the different flows that each major streaming app offered. We concentrated on what made a sequence of steps simplest and most straightforward to a user to take account of for our redesign. Most importantly, we took notes on what confused or bothered users to know what to avoid for our redesign.

After interviewing different users, we gathered all the information and determined which streaming app had the best user workflow. We did comparisons for different scenario and compared step-by-step what made a workflow better than another app. We would then take the best workflow of each scenario and incorporate it into our design.

For example, we compared the workflow for Spotify and Youtube on creating a playlist before browsing for songs to add. For Splotify, users can create a playlist in two ways. 1) Users can go to their playlist page and click on the create playlist button. 2) Users find a song they like and chooses the add the song to playlist option, where they are given the choice to add the song to an existing playlist or create a new one. On the other hand, Youtube only allows users to create a playlist by adding a song to their playlist and is then given the choice to add to existing or create new. Users cannot create a new playlist when accessing their playlist page, which is confusing if users are looking for this option.

Here is our final design. This design will let users have a better experience on Playlist Management.

We did some hard final decisions to get this design.

  1. The biggest decision we made for this design is to add the music control panel to the bottom of the screen. As a music app, we think it’s important to let users control the music they are listening at anytime. For the homepage, if users click play button, the app will play a recommended song based on the user's’ listening history.
  2. For the main navigation bar, we decided to go with the on Spotify has. Looking at the one youtube provides, Subscription may not be used that often. Groove’s navigation bar is not compact and not as easy to access (must click on sandwiched navigation bar button to access the bar). Spotify’s navigation bar provides the most useful buttons in a very simple, compact form.
  3. We decided to have to make the option buttons to dropdown lists instead of a separated page because we think it’s more user friendly to reduce the jumps between pages. For instance, while using Spotify’s app, we found it very distracting to have menus appear all over the place when trying to find features we wanted to use.
  4. During our interviews, we heard some opinions about rearranging the order of the songs within playlists, and we think it’s a good idea. Usually the playlists can support shuffle play, in-order play, and repetition of one song. However, they don’t allow users to change the order of playlist. Therefore, in our design, We add the features for user to drag the songs in the playlist to reorder them. In this case, when their tastes change a little bit, they can always have their favorite songs at the top of playlists. In this way, when they open the playlist, they can have their favorite songs immediately.
  5. We decided to let the button to change to red if they’ve been clicked to remind users of what task they are in a submenu for. It’s often confusing to be unable to see what action you’re doing in case you leave the app for a bit. Youtube has this issue when managing playlists.
  6. We decided to remove the edit and create a new Playlist button in the list of playlists page. We checked most of the music apps, and the only operations you can perform on this page are ‘create playlist’ and ‘delete playlist’. Therefore, instead of a separate page, we decided to add a create button and delete button to the page. To prevent the accidental clicking of these buttons, we added a confirmation page for deletion. This will help reduce the number of clicks needed to complete the same task.
  7. Instead of a big green button for shuffle play, we decided to use more commonly used icons to achieve the same purpose, and we added the support for repeatedly playing one song in case the user really loves one song.
  8. We decided to add the star icon in the detailed playlist page to allow users to mark playlists as favorites easily. Instead of putting this option into the dropdown list, we made it easily accessible to reduce the number of clicks needed to perform this operation.
